Analysis modules

What InvestIQ checks

01

Company profile

Shows the latest available company identity, sector, industry, and core balance-sheet context.

02

Historical financials

Charts annual revenue, net income, and assets from the local fundamentals dataset.

03

Financial ratios

Explains valuation, profitability, leverage, liquidity, and margin ratios with simple status colors.

04

Peer comparison

Compares a company with industry and sector peers using chart-ready normalized values.

05

Valuation signal

Uses the locally trained classifier to classify a ticker as undervalued, fairly valued, or overvalued.

06

Backtest

Runs a simple educational strategy using local daily prices and annual fundamentals.

Local files used by InvestIQ

Annual company financial statements used for revenue, income, assets, equity, debt, EPS, and ratio inputs.

Company list data/raw/securities.csv

Company name, ticker, sector, and industry reference data.

Prices data/raw/prices-split-adjusted.csv

Historical split-adjusted daily prices used for year-end prices and the educational backtest.

data/sp500_prices.parquet

Cleaned local Parquet files used by the backend at runtime.

Local data, clear limits.

InvestIQ uses local CSV source files converted into local Parquet files. It does not call live market APIs, SEC EDGAR, Wikipedia, or yfinance at runtime. The data is historical and may not reflect current market conditions.

Educational use only.

The analysis is designed for learning fundamental analysis and Python web development. It is not investment advice, a recommendation, or a real-time trading system.
